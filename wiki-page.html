The Brown Bear

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is a large bear species found across Eurasia and North America. its conservation status is currently "least concern" There are many subspecies within the brown bear species. including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

Adult brown bears are powerful,top-of-the-food chain predators, but much of their diet consists of nuts, berries, fruit, leaves, and roots. Bears also eat other animals, from rodents to moose. Despite their enormous size, brown bears are extremely fast ,having been clocked at speeds of 30 miles per hour.
